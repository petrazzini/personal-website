Bio

I grew up in Maçambará, a city in the middle of the Pampas, known for it’s rice industry, a song about a horse and Mano Lima, a icon in traditional Gaúcho Music.

I moved to Araranguá when i was 13yo, where i started studying about art, that led me to graphic design, something i fell in love with.

Graduated in Visual communication, i started working with Interface design when i was 16yo, stablishing visual patterns and components for a POS system.

At TW I had the opportunity to work on all products of the company, organizing my time between designing the software and building brand assets, there i learned how to work with limitations, since our product, at the time, was run in older and slow computers at regional stores.

3 years later I joined Organizze, one of the most influential budget manager app in Brazil, there i designed the new brand, reviewed and redesigned the whole mobile experience, also helping build other products from scratch.

Today i run a small design firm, called Stoika with some of my best friends, that I’m lucky enought to call them partners.

At Stoika we’re helping big corps, like JBS, and small emerging startups, like Alice, shape the future of their businesses digitally.

Selected clients

Unidas

I was part of the discovery stage of Acelero, Unidas backed marketplace for premium used vehicles.

Acelero is one of the first platforms where you can buy a car from home, in Brazil we have a lot of bureaucracy to buy and sell cars. Throught Acelero they want to challenge that, and make the whole process of buying a used car online easier and safer.

I was invited by Bossabox team to build the first patterns and stablish how the brand would behave in a digital enviroment.

Meet Unidas

Deskfy

Deskfy is a brand management app that helps companies like Audi and Domino’s boost their marketing strategies giving them consistency and a place to centralize all their brand assets.

They were one of the first clients of Stoika, in 2019 we started a partnership with them to maintain their product design. I was lucky enought to be responsible for their project during almost a year.

Today, they are building their own design team, and growing even faster. I’m pretty happy for being part of this moment with them.

Meet Deskfy
